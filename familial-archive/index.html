Plant: Onion [Allium Cepa]

Onions originated from the regions that are now known as Central Asia and the Middle East. The plants were then brought to the Americas by European colonizers during the 16th century. According to the National Onion Association, Onions were cultivated because of their versatility and adaptability to many climates, as well as their medicinal properties. They are good for digestion, can help prevent thirst, can reduce inflammation, and cure headaches.

My family is from the Southern part of the United States-- specifically South Carolina, Georgia, and Kentucky. Many dishes use the flavor of onions to season the food. Some of my favorite dishes that put onions to use are: Chili, Chicken & Dumplings, Collard Greens, Jambalaya, and Salmon & Grits. The following recipes and stories incorporate Onions in various forms, whether its dried and used as seasoning or sautéed for flavor.

This ‘archive’ of sorts focuses on three of my family members: my Cousin Napoleon, my Mom, and my Dad. Their relation to food and what they’ve passed on to my sister and I is discussed in each section through anecdotes and parts of the interviews I held with them. I also want to start this off by saying that I don’t measure when I cook, I go by feeling. I like to joke that I stop when my ancestors tell me to! So feel free to add more or less to the measurements in the recipes.

Below is a sketch I created in p5. The idea of this sketch was to generate an abstract archive of the familial research I've been doing with color. All of the colors you see within the sketch are from actual photos I've either taken or found on line that have some sort of connection with my heritage. I then indexed this collection of photos for their RBG and HEX Codes to put in my own personal database. All of the research I've been doing feels like I've been 'stitching' together pieces of my identity to bring me a sense of comfort, home, and belonging. I thought the best way to represent that would be making a quilt!

Some of the colors are from the multistep process of prepping veggies and browning meat for Chilli. Some of the colors are pictures of my Mom and I's favorite riverside park in Atlanta, GA. One of them may or may not be the very special cans of Canada Dry Bold Ginger Ale my Cousin Napoleon and I drank while I interviewed him. Regardless, I think this was the best way to share this very intimate journey I've been on this semester in a way that honors my privacy but also shares that vulnerability and discovery with others.
